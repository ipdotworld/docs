Every trend on ip.world uses one of two ownership models. The model is set at creation and determines how verification, fees, and governance work.

## Creator trends

A creator trend maps to a clear real-world owner: a person, a brand, a project, or an authorized representative.

- The owner links an X or TikTok account during verification.
- Launching a token at creation is optional - the trend can exist before the first token.
- Verification proves **who owns it**. The verified owner claims the trend and unlocks their allocation and fee share.

Creator trend economics: the verified owner receives **3% of every token's supply** vested over 3 months, plus the owner share of trading fees on every token under the trend.

## Community trends

A community trend is a bottom-up movement owned by its holders rather than a single entity.

- Launching a token at creation is **required** - the trend needs a token to accrue holders.
- Verification is **milestone-based** rather than identity-based: the trend's top token needs to hit market-cap, holder-count, and age thresholds before the trend is eligible.
- Once verified, holders govern the trend's treasury through Snapshot and a Gnosis Safe multisig.

The trend page shows milestone progress as the community makes it.

## Quick test

Use the creator model when there is a real person, brand, or rights-holder behind the idea. Use the community model when the idea emerged bottom-up and no single owner can claim it.
