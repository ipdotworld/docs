## The ten-second version

**ip.world** is a launchpad for internet culture. Anyone can create a token around a meme, a trend, a character, or an idea. Trading those tokens generates fees, and those fees flow to the people who create the culture, hold the tokens, govern the community, and run the protocol.

We call the category **culture markets**: internet culture and financial markets in one product.

### What makes ip.world different

- **UGC rewards.** Content creators earn from trading fees when they post about a token on X or TikTok. You create the content; the fees that fund you are the same fees the market pays.
- **Community treasuries.** Every token has a treasury funded by trading fees. Holders vote on how those funds are spent. See [Community Treasury](/user-guides/manage/treasury-and-proposals).
- **Verification.** Real owners can verify their trend and claim the fee routes, vested allocation, and trust badge that come with it. See [Verification overview](/user-guides/verification/verification-overview).

## Two objects

ip.world is organized around two things:

- **Trend** - the canonical page for an idea: a meme, a storyline, a brand, a movement. One trend can carry many tokens.
- **Token** - a single tradable market under a trend. It has a price, a bonding curve, a trade panel, holders, and governance.

Read [Coin Types](/user-guides/coin-types) for how creator-type and community-type tokens differ.
