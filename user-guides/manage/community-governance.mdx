Community trends are governed by their holders. Governance has three parts:

- A **canonical voting token** - the first token launched under the trend. Holding this token is what gives you voting rights.
- **Snapshot** - the proposal and voting surface, embedded into the ip.world UI.
- **Gnosis Safe** - the multisig that executes proposals once they pass.

## Who can vote and propose

You need to hold the canonical voting token in your connected wallet. Voting power is weighted by balance.

## What you can do

- **Vote** - For, Against, or Abstain on open proposals.
- **Create proposals** - submit spending, parameter, or policy proposals once you pass the proposal threshold.
- **Execute** - after a proposal passes, the multisig signers execute the on-chain action.

![Treasury proposals figure](/images/token-treasury.svg)

## Pass rule

A proposal passes when:

1. **YES > NO**, and
2. **YES ≥ REQUIRED_YES**, where **REQUIRED_YES = 10% + NO** (base 10% + one vote of YES for every vote of NO).

In plain English: controversial proposals need a disproportionately large YES to pass. A proposal with 5% NO only needs 15% YES; a proposal with 20% NO needs 30% YES.

<Tip>
  The pass rule is designed so close or contested votes do not scrape through on low turnout.
</Tip>
