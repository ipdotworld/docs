ip.world runs five parallel reward programs. They share terminology but pay out from different surfaces, on different cadences, to different people. Read this page before you tell anyone how to earn.

## The five programs

| Program | What it is | Paid from | Where you see it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Points** | Season score from quests, trading volume, and milestones | Off-chain leaderboard | Leaderboard, profile |
| **Referrals** | Points and fee-share earned by inviting new users | Points + protocol fee share | Referral program, profile |
| **UGC rewards** | Payouts to creators whose posts drive attention | UGC share of trading fees | Token page, UGC surfaces |
| **Holder rewards** | Payouts to wallets that hold the token | Holder share of trading fees | Token page |
| **Owner / treasury** | Payouts to verified owners or community treasuries | Trading fees routed to owner / treasury | Owner dashboard, [Community Treasury](/user-guides/manage/treasury-and-proposals) |

![Earning systems diagram](/images/diagram-earning-flow.svg)

## Disambiguation

Points and fee-share are not the same thing. **Points** are an off-chain score that rank you on the leaderboard and unlock season rewards. **Fee-share** is on-chain - real IP, USDC, or token earnings from trading activity.

Creator-type owner earnings and UGC earnings are also separate programs. A verified creator-type owner receives a fixed cut of trading fees routed to their wallet. A UGC creator receives fees only for posts that meet the indexing rules and pass moderation.
